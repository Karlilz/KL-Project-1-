Insulin-to-Carb Ratios

The insulin-to-carb ratio is the grams of carbohydrates (CHO) one unit of rapid-acting insulin covers to make sure that blood glucose stays within the desired limit.

The carb ratio is often set by a healthcare provider when diabetes is diagnosed but needs updating regularly. A diabetics ratio may change over the course of their lifetime or even throughout the day. Some people have one ratio for breakfast and a different ratio for lunch and dinner.

For example, someone might take 1 unit for every 10 grams of carb. Others might take 1:15.
